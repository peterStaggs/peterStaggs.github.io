Bangazon Storefront is a basic vendor site that allows users to login, buy and sell products in an open market environment. I helped built this as a member of a five-person team for Nashville Software School's back-end Python course. Over the course of a month, we used Agile/Scrum sprints to shape our approach to writing tickets, GitHub workflow, and test-driven development. In the final week we shifted to a more free-form Kanban approach. This project was built entirely with Django, and I personally built the Registration/Login features, the ability to post products for sale, extended the built-in Django User model to include additional values, and rebuilt the Order form after a requested pivot from our notional product owner.
